# RNA Mechanisms & Molecular Computing

## RNA Binding Proteins (RBPs)
RNA binding proteins (RBPs) are a diverse class of proteins that specifically interact with RNA molecules through one or more RNA-binding domains, regulating virtually every aspect of RNA metabolism and function. RBPs bind single-stranded RNA, double-stranded RNA, or structured RNA motifs via domains such as RNA recognition motif (RRM), double-stranded RNA-binding motif (dsRBM), KH domain, zinc knuckles, and PUF domains. They perform multiple roles:
- Pre-mRNA processing
- mRNA stability and turnover
- mRNA localization
- Translation regulation
- RNA quality control
- Viral RNA interactions

## RNA Cap
RNA cap is a 7-methylguanosine structure added to the 5′ end of eukaryotic mRNA and some viral RNAs shortly after transcription initiation. The cap serves multiple functions:
- Protects mRNA from 5′ exonucleases.
- Facilitates nuclear export via CBC (cap-binding complex).
- Recruits initiation factors (eIF4F) for cap-dependent translation.
- Enhances splicing, polyadenylation, and mRNA stability.

## Catalytic RNA (Ribozymes)
RNA, catalytic refers to RNA molecules that possess intrinsic enzymatic activity (ribozymes), capable of catalyzing biochemical reactions without protein assistance. Catalytic RNAs demonstrate that RNA can function both as genetic material and as a biocatalyst, supporting the RNA world hypothesis for the origin of life. Major natural ribozymes include:
- Self-splicing introns (group I and II)
- RNase P
- Ribosome (peptidyl transferase center)
- Small nucleolytic ribozymes (hammerhead, hairpin, HDV, VS, glmS)
- Artificial ribozymes (Diels–Alderase, ligase, RNA polymerase)

## RNA Chaperones
RNA chaperone is a protein that assists RNA in folding into its correct secondary and tertiary structures, preventing misfolding or aggregation without providing specific catalytic activity or stable incorporation into the final RNA structure. They are ATP-independent and act primarily through transient interactions. Major RNA chaperones include hnRNP proteins, Cold-shock proteins, DEAD-box RNA helicases, Hfq, and La protein.

## RNA Codons
RNA codons are triplets of nucleotides in mRNA that specify individual amino acids or translation signals during protein synthesis. The genetic code consists of 64 possible codons (4³ combinations of A, C, G, U):
- 61 sense codons encode 20 amino acids.
- 3 stop codons (UAA, UAG, UGA) signal translation termination.
- 1 start codon (AUG) codes for methionine and initiates translation.

## RNA Computing
RNA computer refers to a molecular computing system that uses RNA molecules as computational elements to perform logic operations, signal processing, or decision-making tasks at the molecular level. RNA computers exploit RNA’s ability to form specific base-paired structures, catalyze reactions (ribozymes), and bind ligands or proteins in a programmable manner. Key approaches include:
- RNA logic gates (ribozymes or aptamers that switch activity upon ligand binding)
- RNA circuits (cascading riboregulators or toehold-mediated strand displacement)
- In vitro selection
- In vivo RNA computing

## RNA-dependent RNA polymerase (RdRP)
RdRP is an enzyme that synthesizes RNA from an RNA template, essential for replication and transcription of RNA viruses and certain cellular processes. They lack proofreading exonuclease activity, leading to high error rates.
